Difference: Bayesian CIs

  • Both Bayesian and non-Bayesian models produce ‚ÄòCIs‚Äô, but use different words for the ‚ÄòC‚Äô:
    • ‚ÄòConfidence intervals‚Äô vs.
    • Bayesian ‚Äòcredible intervals‚Äô (sometimes ‚Äòcompatible intervals‚Äô)
  • Interpretation: according to our data and model, there is a 95% change the parameter is in the interval.
  • There‚Äôs nothing special about 95%, we can use any other number we like (often more than one).

  • This ‚Äòhalf-eye‚Äô plot shows 50%, 70% and 90% intervals around the mean.
  • More on making these plots next time!

Flexible estimates

  • Repeat lots of times to get a distribution.

Background: probability

  • The alternative to Bayesianism is ‚Äòfrequentism‚Äô.
  • Core difference: how they use probability
  • Frequentists: probability quantifies the reliability of the method.
  • Bayesians: probability quantifies uncertainty.
  • Both are well motivated ideas!

Background: probability (cont.)

  • Frequentist probability is connected to proportions of samples, e.g.:
    • ‚ÄòIf this analysis were repeated over and over again, we would draw the wrong conclusion 5% of the time.‚Äô
    • ‚ÄòIf you sampled NZE speakers born in 1937, 10% of would regularly use ‚Äúkia ora‚Äù as a greeting.‚Äô
  • Bayesian‚Äôs use probability for uncertainty:
    • ‚ÄòGiven the data and model, we are 90% sure that the effect of this variable is greater than zero‚Äô
    • ‚ÄòThe probability that language X and language Y are in the same family is 65%‚Äô (‚Ä¶what would you sample?)
  • It‚Äôs very hard to understand the Bayesian examples in terms of sampling.
    • ‚ÄòIf universes were as plenty as blackberries‚Ä¶‚Äô (CS Peirce)

Confidence vs. credible (again)

  • Standard 95% confidence intervals: i.e. ‚Äòfrequentist‚Äô
    • If we repeat this method, the parameter we‚Äôre estimating will be inside the confidence interval 95% of the time.
    • We‚Äôre ‚Äòsampling‚Äô applications of the method.
    • It‚Äôs not a claim about this exact confidence interval!
  • Bayesian 95% credible intervals:
    • Given the data and model, we reckon, with 95% confidence, that the parameter is somewhere in here!
    • It‚Äôs a claim about this exact interval.
    • Easier to interpret, but we‚Äôve changed the topic.

NB

  • Bayesianism is not ‚ÄòStatistics 2.0‚Äô
  • There‚Äôs nothing especially subjective about Bayesianism.
    • Bayesian methods use probability to express some subjective elements.
    • Frequentists often put subjective elements into model assumptions etc.
  • You don‚Äôt have to be Bayesian
    • ‚Ä¶but you should understand it enough to read and review articles.
